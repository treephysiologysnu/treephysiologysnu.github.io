Aim of this study

Climate change due to the global warming has had a significant impact on plant phenology. To predict and monitor the change of plant phenology, long-term collected data is required. Increasing research in plant phenology has made possible various techniques to measure phenology data automatically. As one of the most popular technique, digital repeat photography are used, and online phenology networks has been established worldwide in United States (PhenoCam), Europe (EPN), Japan (PEN) and so on.

The aim of our study is to develop and nationalize an automatic phenology observation system including detection algorithms for various phenological events at individual tree level. For the individual observation equipment, a small and affordable cheap raspberry-pi was used.
